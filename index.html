About Her

Semeen Ali was born in Agra and completed her schooling from St. Mary's Convent, Allahabad. She completed her graduation in English Honours from Miranda House and completed her post-graduation and M.Phil from Department of English, University of Delhi. She is currently a PhD scholar at the Department of English, University of Delhi.

She started writing poetry when she was 8 years old and her first poem was published at the age of 10. Her first book Broken Barriers was published in 2008 from Writers Workshop and she has four books of poetry to her credit. Her second book - Rose and Ashes (Writers Workshop, 2009) was nominated for the Muse India Young Writers Award at the Hyderabad Literary Festival, 2012 and got the Jury's Commendation Award. Her third book Origins (2012) was published from The Poets Printery, South Africa. Her latest collection of poems, Transitions was published from Writers Workshop, 2014.

She was invited to read from her works at the Hyderabad Literary Festival, 2015.

Her poems have featured in journals like Muse India, The Hudson Poetry, The Four Quarters Magazine, and The Taj Mahal Review. Her poems have featured in anthologies - The Dance of the Peacock, Anthology of English poetry from India published by Hidden Brooks Press, Canada in 2013 and Suvarnareka, Anthology of Indian Women Poets published by The Poetry Society of India, 2014.

Her poems are featuring in a forthcoming Anthology of Indian Poetry in Greek to be released in 2016.

She has recently edited an anthology - Dilli, an Anthology of Women Poets of Delhi published by Poets Printery, South Africa, 2014.

She has written several book reviews for the The Book Review India.

She is currently guest editing Muse India's 64th (November-December 2015 Issue) with focus on 'L'Ecriture Feminine'.

She is currently editing an upcoming anthology along with poet and writer, Nabina Das which focuses on poets who were growing up in the 1980s and the 1990s.
